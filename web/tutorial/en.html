OpenCurtainLab Guide

Using the tester on cameras

This guide explains how to place OpenCurtainLab in or near the film plane, how to choose the correct measurement mode, how to run repeatable tests, and how to interpret the results when servicing cameras.

Connector safety

Warning: The lamp connector is not an audio connector. Do not connect headphones, audio equipment, microphones, or camera flash cables to the lamp socket. The lamp socket can carry 12 V and is only intended for the OpenCurtainLab lamp.

The flash-sync connector and the lamp connector are separate functions. Check the symbols on the housing before connecting cables.

Prepare the camera

  1. Set the camera to the shutter speed you want to test.
  2. Open the camera back or place the tester where the film or sensor would normally sit.
  3. Use a steady, even light source through the lens mount or front opening.
  4. Make sure no shutter blade, dark slide, mirror, or test fixture covers the sensor row before release.
  5. For mechanical shutters, fire the camera a few times before recording final values.
Avoid flickering lamps and PWM LED light sources. They can create irregular sensor signals that look like shutter problems.
For service work, measure each important speed several times. A single release can be misleading on old mechanical cameras.

Place the tester

Place the sensor row as close as possible to the film plane. The row should cross the image area in the same direction that the shutter travels.

Focal-plane shutter
Align the row with the curtain travel. All five sensors should be illuminated before the shutter is released.
Leaf or central shutter
Place the tester in the image area and choose the central shutter mode. Curtain speed charts are not used for central shutters.
Lens shutter in a camera
Keep the aperture open enough that all sensors receive light. If the aperture clips the row, the measurement can look incomplete.

Choose the measurement mode

Select only the measurement geometry. The WebUI detects the travel direction from the first sensor that opens.

VerticalFor shutters whose opening travels along the vertical sensor axis.
HorizontalFor shutters whose opening travels along the horizontal sensor axis.
Central shutterUse this for leaf shutters and other shutters that open around the centre instead of travelling as curtains.
The detected direction is derived from the order of the sensor edges.

Run a test

  1. Set the target speed on the device or in the connected interface.
  2. Wait until the device is ready.
  3. Release the camera shutter normally.
  4. Repeat the same speed several times before judging a repair.
  5. Test fast speeds, medium speeds, and slow speeds separately because they often fail for different reasons.
If a sensor is already covered before release, the device cannot start a clean measurement. Check alignment and make sure light reaches all sensors.

Read the result

Measured exposureThe effective exposure time calculated from the sensor timings.
Deviation EVDifference from the selected target speed in stops. Positive values mean the exposure was longer; negative values mean it was shorter.
SpreadVariation between sensors. A small spread means the frame is exposed evenly. A large spread points to capping, uneven slit width, or uneven curtain travel.
Total open timeThe time between the first part of the frame opening and the last part closing. This is useful for flash sync checks.
Measurement hintA warning about the captured release, for example incomplete sensor coverage or a timeout. Treat it as diagnostic information, not automatically as a device fault.
A camera can be consistently off but repeatable. That usually means adjustment is needed. Random variation usually means friction, sticking parts, weak springs, or an unstable slow-speed mechanism.

Use the charts

The timeline shows when each sensor opened and closed. It is the fastest way to spot capping or an incomplete run.

The V(x) chart shows local curtain speed over the sensor position. The measured points are calculated between neighbouring sensors. The line may be interpolated for easier reading, but the measured points are the important data.

Smooth line
The curtain moves evenly across the frame.
Rising or falling trend
The curtain speeds up or slows down during travel.
Strong opening/closing difference
The slit width changes across the frame, which can create uneven exposure.

Assess repairs

All speeds too slow or too fastCheck general tension, timing adjustment, or shutter regulation.
Fast speeds unevenCheck curtain tension, brake action, slit width, and curtain parallelism.
Slow speeds irregularInspect the slow-speed escapement, old lubricant, and delayed release mechanisms.
Only part of the sensor row is exposedLook for capping, shutter bounce, blocked travel, or incorrect tester placement.
Results change after repeated releasesOld lubrication, sticky bearings, or weak springs may be warming up or freeing temporarily.
After adjustment, compare several repeated measurements at the same speed. Repeatability is often more important than one perfect release.

Check flash sync

  1. Connect the camera flash sync contact to the tester.
  2. Use the camera's X-sync setting or the speed recommended by the service manual.
  3. Run a measurement and check whether the flash event happens while the frame is fully open.
If the flash event is outside the total opening time, the sync contact may be early, late, dirty, or mechanically misadjusted.
Do not plug the flash-sync cable into the lamp socket. The lamp socket is a 12 V lamp output and is not an audio or flash-sync connector.

For focal-plane shutters, flash sync should be assessed at or below the camera's specified sync speed. Faster speeds often expose the frame as a travelling slit.

Finish the test

After the camera has been tested, export the CSV file and keep it with the repair notes. It documents the measured speeds, deviations, spread, flash result, and remarks for later comparison.
